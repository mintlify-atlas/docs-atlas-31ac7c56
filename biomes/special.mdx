Special biomes in Origen override the normal biome distribution system, allowing them to generate in unexpected locations with specific conditions.

## Special Biome Types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sinkholes">
    <Note type="warning">
      Currently disabled in the latest version.
    </Note>

    Sinkholes can be found anywhere in the world, ignoring the usual biome cell spawns. However, they are quite rare. They reach down to Y -40 and have steep cliffs and are usually stretched at an angle.

    **Generation:**
    - Can spawn anywhere in the world
    - Ignores normal cellular biome distribution
    - Very rare occurrence rate
    - Reaches down to Y -40
    - Features steep cliff walls
    - Usually stretched at an angle

    ### Sinkhole Variants

    There are currently 3 sinkhole variations:

    **Jungle Sinkhole**
    - Warm surface environment
    - Big jungle trees covering the bottom
    - Lush vegetation throughout

    **Forest Sinkhole**
    - Mossy stone walls
    - Diverse mix of tree types
    - Covers the bottom of the sinkhole

    **Frozen Sinkhole**
    - Cold climate variant
    - Snowy surface covering
    - Ice spikes on the floor
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Special Generation Mechanics

Unlike regular biomes that follow the cellular noise distribution system, special biomes use independent generation rules:

- **Independent Spawning** - Not tied to biome cells or continental values
- **Rarity Control** - Spawn frequency controlled separately from other biomes
- **Override Behavior** - Can replace or modify existing biomes
- **Unique Placement** - May have specific terrain or location requirements

<Note>
Special biomes are designed to create rare and memorable discoveries during exploration, adding elements of surprise to world generation.
</Note>
